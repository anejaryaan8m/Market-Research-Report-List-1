<p><p>The rheological modifier market is divided into two main types: organic and inorganic rheological modifiers. Organic rheological modifiers are derived from natural or synthetic polymers, enhancing viscosity and flow properties in applications like paints, coatings, and personal care products. In contrast, inorganic rheological modifiers include materials such as clays and silica, primarily used to stabilize and thicken formulations in industries like construction and food. Both types play crucial roles in optimizing product performance and application efficiency.</p></p>

<p><p>The patient monitor market is categorized into three main types based on acuity levels. High-acuity monitors are designed for critically ill patients in intensive care units, providing comprehensive real-time monitoring and complex data analysis. Mid-acuity monitors cater to patients with moderate health issues, often found in telemetry units, offering essential vital sign tracking. Low-acuity monitors are used for stable patients in less intensive settings, focusing on basic vital sign measurements. Each type serves distinct healthcare needs and environments.</p></p>

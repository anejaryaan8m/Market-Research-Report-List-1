<p><p>The rear axle steering system market is segmented into two main types: single/central actuator and dual actuator systems. Single/central actuator systems utilize one central mechanism to control the rear axle's movement, allowing for simpler design and integration. In contrast, dual actuator systems employ two independent actuators for improved precision and responsiveness, enabling more dynamic handling and stability. These systems enhance vehicle maneuverability, particularly in tight spaces, and are increasingly adopted in various automotive applications, including passenger cars and commercial vehicles.</p></p>
